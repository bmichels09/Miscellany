Road to 36,000

The CDC estimates that in the 10 flu seasons preceding the emergence of COVID-19, influenza killed 36,000 Americans per year, making it the deadliest contagious disease in the US. Since we use vaccines to keep that number from being higher, we can consider 36,000 as the highest number of annual deaths from a contagious disease that's socially acceptable. Therefore, to go back to "normal", we need to get annual COVID-19 deaths under 36,000 (or at least close to 36,000).

This map tracks each state's progress toward that goal, using data from the last 14 days. 36,000 deaths in one year is equivalent to roughly 100 deaths per day, which is equivalent to roughly 0.3 daily deaths for every million Americans. States below that threshold are colored green and do not need to take measures to lower the number of COVID-19 deaths.
